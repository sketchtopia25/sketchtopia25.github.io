Sketchtopia: Asynchronous Multimodal Sketch Communication

Mohd Hozaifa Khan, Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla
IIIT Hyderabad
Paper (CVPR 2025) arXiv Code (GitHub) Dataset

Abstract

We introduce Sketchtopia, a large-scale dataset and AI framework designed to explore goal-driven, multimodal communication through asynchronous interactions in a Pictionary-inspired setting. Sketchtopia captures natural human interactions—including freehand sketches, open-ended text guesses, and iconic feedback gestures—showcasing the complex dynamics of cooperative communication under constraints. The dataset features over 20,000 gameplay sessions from 916 players, comprising over 263,000 sketches, 10,000 erasures, 56,000 guesses, and 19,400 iconic feedback instances. We introduce foundational multimodal agents with capabilities for generative sketching, guess generation, and asynchronous communication. Sketchtopia also includes 800 human-agent sessions for benchmarking the agents. We introduce novel metrics to characterize collaborative success, responsiveness to feedback, and inter-agent asynchronous communication. Sketchtopia pushes the boundaries of multimodal AI, establishing a new benchmark for studying asynchronous, goal-oriented interactions between humans and AI agents.

Meet Sketchtopia Agents

DRAWBOT: The Generative Sketcher

DRAWBOT, our foundational agent for asynchronous sketching. It's built upon Stable Diffusion and adapter modules, allowing it to:

  • Generate sketches based on textual descriptions.
  • Respond to guesses and feedback by iteratively refining its sketches.
  • Incorporate iconic feedback (👍, 👎, ❓) to improve communication.

GUESSBOT: The Intelligent Guesser

GUESSBOT, our intelligent guessing agent, embodies the Guesser role. It uses a retrieval-based framework to:

  • Analyze the current state of the sketch canvas.
  • Consider the history of guesses and feedback.
  • Generate relevant and timely textual guesses.

Benchmarking: Novel Metrics for Asynchronous AI

Asynchronous Action Overlap (AAO)

AAO measures the degree of overlap between DRAWBOT's sketching actions and GUESSBOT's guessing actions. A higher AAO indicates more natural, human-like asynchronous communication, where actions can occur simultaneously.

Feedback Responsiveness Score (FRS)

FRS quantifies how effectively DRAWBOT responds to GUESSBOT's feedback (👍, 👎, ❓). A higher FRS indicates that DRAWBOT is successfully interpreting and incorporating feedback to improve its sketches.

Multimodal Action Timing Similarity (MATS)

MATS compares the timing patterns of agent actions (sketching, guessing, feedback) to those observed in human-human interactions. A higher MATS score suggests more natural and human-like timing.
