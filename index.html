Sketchtopia: Advancing Multimodal AI with Asynchronous Sketch Communication

A new benchmark dataset and foundational agents for exploring human-AI collaboration through drawing and language.
Mohd Hozaifa Khan, Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla
IIIT Hyderabad
Paper (CVPR 2025) arXiv Code (GitHub) Dataset

Abstract

We introduce Sketchtopia, a large-scale dataset and AI framework designed to explore goal-driven, multimodal communication through asynchronous interactions in a Pictionary-inspired setting. Sketchtopia captures natural human interactions‚Äîincluding freehand sketches, open-ended text guesses, and iconic feedback gestures‚Äîshowcasing the complex dynamics of cooperative communication under constraints. The dataset features over 20,000 gameplay sessions from 916 players, comprising over 263,000 sketches, 10,000 erasures, 56,000 guesses, and 19,400 iconic feedback instances. We introduce foundational multimodal agents with capabilities for generative sketching, guess generation, and asynchronous communication. Sketchtopia also includes 800 human-agent sessions for benchmarking the agents. We introduce novel metrics to characterize collaborative success, responsiveness to feedback, and inter-agent asynchronous communication. Sketchtopia pushes the boundaries of multimodal AI, establishing a new benchmark for studying asynchronous, goal-oriented interactions between humans and AI agents.

Meet the Sketchtopia Agents

DRAWBOT: The Generative Sketcher

DRAWBOT is our generative agent that has been developed to asynchronously sketch based on textual descriptions. It leverages state-of-the-art generative models.

  • Generate sketches based on textual descriptions.
  • Respond to guesses and feedback by iteratively refining its sketches.

GUESSBOT: The Intelligent Guesser

GUESSBOT, our intelligent guessing agent, embodies the Guesser role and is built upon a retreival framework to provide accurate and timely guesses.

  • Analyze the current state of the sketch canvas.
  • Consider the history of guesses and feedback.
  • Generate relevant and timely textual guesses.

Benchmarking: Novel Metrics for Asynchronous AI

AAO

Asynchronous Action Overlap

Measures the degree of simultaneity in agent actions, reflecting natural, asynchronous communication flow.

FRS

Feedback Responsiveness Score

Quantifies how effectively DRAWBOT incorporates feedback (üëç, üëé, ‚ùì) to improve its sketches.

MATS

Multimodal Action Timing Similarity

Compares the timing patterns of agent actions with human-human interactions to assess naturalness.
